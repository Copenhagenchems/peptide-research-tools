# Peptide Research Glossary

Key terms for peptide research. Full guides at [CopenhagenChems](https://copenhagenchems.com/research/).

## A–G

**Actin** — Structural protein; TB-500 upregulates actin to accelerate cell migration in tissue repair.

**Angiogenesis** — Formation of new blood vessels. BPC-157's primary repair mechanism. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/bpc-157-gut-healing-protocol/)

**AMPK** — AMP-activated protein kinase; energy sensor activated by MOTS-C. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/mots-c-mitochondrial-peptide-anti-aging/)

**Bacteriostatic Water (BAC)** — Sterile water with 0.9% benzyl alcohol; preferred for peptide reconstitution. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/how-to-reconstitute-store-peptides/)

**BDNF** — Brain-derived neurotrophic factor; upregulated by BPC-157 and Semax.

**Bioavailability** — Proportion of a substance reaching systemic circulation. Oral peptides have near-zero bioavailability. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/oral-peptides-dont-work-injectable-only/)

**GLP-1** — Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor; targeted by semaglutide, tirzepatide, and retatrutide. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/glp-1-peptides-weight-loss-beyond-ozempic/)

**GHK-Cu** — Glycine-histidine-lysine copper complex; regulates 4,000+ genes. Must be used alone. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/ghk-cu-peptide-gene-control-protocol/)

## H–M

**Half-life** — Time for plasma concentration to halve. Varies widely: BPC-157 ~4h subQ, CJC-1295 DAC ~6-8 days.

**Insulin syringe** — Standard delivery device; 1ml = 100 units. Most peptide doses measured in units.

**Lyophilized** — Freeze-dried form; stable for long-term storage. Requires reconstitution before use.

**Mitochondrial peptide** — Peptide encoded in mitochondrial DNA (e.g. MOTS-C). [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/mots-c-mitochondrial-peptide-anti-aging/)

**Mucosal regeneration** — Repair of gut lining; primary mechanism of BPC-157 for gut healing. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/bpc-157-gut-healing-protocol/)

## N–Z

**NFκB** — Nuclear factor kappa B; inflammatory signaling pathway modulated by BPC-157 and Thymosin Alpha-1.

**Proteolytic degradation** — Breakdown of peptides by digestive enzymes; why oral peptides don't work systemically. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/oral-peptides-dont-work-injectable-only/)

**RUO** — Research Use Only; regulatory classification for research peptides in EU/US.

**SubQ (Subcutaneous)** — Injection into fat layer under skin; standard route for most research peptides.

**Telomerase** — Enzyme that extends telomeres; activated by Epithalon. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/epithalon-peptide-telomere-longevity-protocol/)

**Telomere** — Protective cap on chromosomes; shortens with age; key anti-aging target.

**Triple agonist** — Compound targeting three receptors (e.g. Retatrutide: GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon). [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/retatrutide-triple-agonist-weight-loss/)

**Wolverine Protocol** — BPC-157 + TB-500 combination for aggressive injury recovery. [→](https://copenhagenchems.com/wolverine-protocol-bpc-157-tb-500-stack/)
